## Coolant / Antifreeze

### Types by Color (general guide)
| Color | Type | Used By | Lifespan |
|---|---|---|---|
| Green | IAT (Inorganic Acid Technology) | Older domestic vehicles (pre-2000) | 2 years / 30,000 mi |
| Orange | OAT / Dex-Cool | GM (1996+), some others | 5 years / 150,000 mi |
| Pink / Red | OAT | Toyota, Lexus, Scion | 5 years / 100,000 mi (SLLC) |
| Blue | OAT/P-OAT | Honda, Acura, some Hyundai | 5-10 years |
| Blue (European) | P-HOAT (Si-OAT) | VW, Audi (G12/G13) | 5 years |
| Purple / Violet | P-HOAT (Si-OAT) | VW, Audi (G12++) | 5 years / 150,000 mi |
| Yellow | HOAT (Hybrid OAT) | Ford, Chrysler, European | 5 years / 100,000 mi |
| Gold | HOAT | Ford (Motorcraft Gold) | 5 years / 100,000 mi |

**NEVER mix different coolant types.** If unsure, flush completely before switching. Mixing can cause gelling and cooling system blockage.

### Mix Ratio
- Standard: **50/50** coolant to distilled water (-34F to 265F protection)
- Extreme cold: **60/40** coolant to water (-62F protection)
- Extreme heat: **40/60** coolant to water (better heat transfer but less freeze protection)
- Pre-mixed products: Ready to use, do not add water

### Typical Capacities
| Vehicle Type | Typical System Capacity |
|---|---|
| 4-cylinder | 6-8 quarts |
| V6 | 10-14 quarts |
| V8 | 14-18 quarts |
| Diesel truck | 20-28 quarts |

## Brake Fluid

### Types
| Spec | Boiling Point (Dry) | Boiling Point (Wet) | Use Case |
|---|---|---|---|
| DOT 3 | 401F | 284F | Basic vehicles, light duty |
| DOT 4 | 446F | 311F | Most modern vehicles, European standard |
| DOT 5.1 | 500F | 356F | High performance, heavy braking |
| DOT 5 (SILICONE) | 500F | 356F | Military, show cars. NOT compatible with DOT 3/4/5.1 |

**Manufacturer requirements:**
- Most domestic: DOT 3 or DOT 4
- BMW: DOT 4 LV (low viscosity)
- Mercedes: DOT 4+ or Super DOT 4
- Most Asian: DOT 3 or DOT 4
- Performance/track: DOT 4 minimum, DOT 5.1 preferred

**Brake fluid is hygroscopic** (absorbs water). Replace every 2-3 years regardless of mileage. Wet boiling point is what matters in real-world use.

**NEVER use DOT 5 (silicone) in a system designed for DOT 3/4.** They are NOT compatible.

## Differential / Gear Oil

### Common Specs
| Application | Typical Spec | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Open differential | 75W-90 GL-5 | Most common |
| Limited-slip differential | 75W-90 GL-5 + friction modifier | MUST add modifier or use LSD-specific fluid |
| Front diff (AWD) | 75W-90 GL-5 or manufacturer specific | |
| Truck axle (heavy duty) | 75W-140 GL-5 | Synthetic recommended for towing |
| Motorcycle final drive | 80W-90 GL-5 | Shaft-drive bikes |

**LIMITED SLIP WARNING**: LSD diffs REQUIRE friction modifier additive or LSD-specific gear oil. Without it, you get chatter on turns. GM = use Posi additive. Ford = use friction modifier.

### Typical Capacities
| Differential | Typical Capacity |
|---|---|
| Car rear diff | 1.5-2.5 pints |
| Truck rear diff (8.8"/9.75") | 4-5 pints |
| Truck rear diff (10.5"/11.5") | 6-7 pints |
| Front diff (AWD truck) | 2-3 pints |

## Grease Types

### Chassis Grease
| Type | Use | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| NLGI #2 (standard) | Ball joints, tie rods, U-joints | Most common chassis grease |
| Moly (MoS2) grease | Ball joints, U-joints, heavy load | Better extreme pressure protection |
| Marine grease | Boat trailers, waterproof applications | Water-resistant formulation |
| White lithium | Door hinges, latches, hood springs | General purpose, clean |
| Silicone brake grease | Caliper slide pins, brake hardware | Does NOT contact rubber seals (non-petroleum) |
| Rubber-safe grease | Caliper piston boots, seals | Will not swell or damage rubber |

### Wheel Bearing Grease
- **Disc brake wheel bearings**: High-temp grease rated 400F+
- **Trailer bearings**: Marine-grade, water-resistant
- **NEVER mix grease types** in the same bearing. Old grease must be completely removed

### Grease Interval
| Application | Interval |
|---|---|
| Ball joints (with zerks) | Every oil change or 5,000 mi |
| Tie rod ends (with zerks) | Every oil change or 5,000 mi |
| U-joints (with zerks) | Every oil change or 5,000 mi |
| Wheel bearings (serviceable) | 30,000 mi or every 2 years |
| 5th wheel / trailer hitch | Before each tow |
